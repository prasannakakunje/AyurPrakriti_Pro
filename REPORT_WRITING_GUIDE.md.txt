# AyurPrakriti Report — Writing Guide (Clinical Tone & Templates)

## 1. Principle (single rule)
**Never use “you.”**  
All clinical observations, interpretations and recommendations must be written in objective, third-person tone.

---

## 2. Structure (required order)
1. Cover (logo, clinic name, generation timestamp)
2. Executive Summary (3–4 short lines; factual)
3. Prakriti (Constitution) — table
4. Vikriti (Current state) — table
5. Psychometric Snapshot — short bullets
6. Integrated Interpretation — combine 3–5 lines (Prakriti + Vikriti + Psych)
7. Dosha-specific Guidance — bullet lists
8. Seasonal Guidance (Ritucharya) — include Vasanta
9. Practical Recommended Sequence (Immediate / This week / This month) — vertical
10. Recommended Work Domains (career) — ranked with rationale
11. Relationship & Health Tips — merged list
12. Appendix / Transformation Plan (optional)
13. Doctor’s Note — highlighted box (optional)
14. Footer — contact & page numbers

---

## 3. Tone & Vocabulary
- Use neutral, factual statements:
  - ✔ "Pitta is elevated."
  - ✔ "Vata imbalance may present with dryness or restlessness."
  - ✖ "You are likely to feel restless."
- Use words: "recommended", "advised", "supports", "favours", "encouraged"
- Avoid imperative commands ("Do X"). Prefer "is recommended" or "is advised".

---

## 4. Dosha wording templates
- Vata guidance:
  - "Warmth and routine support Vata balance."
  - "Gentle oil application and warm meals are advised."
- Pitta guidance:
  - "Cooling practices and calming breathwork support Pitta regulation."
  - "Cooling foods and hydration are recommended."
- Kapha guidance:
  - "Light, dry, and stimulating practices support Kapha reduction."
  - "Avoid heavy, oily, and sugary foods."

---

## 5. Seasonal (Ritucharya) templates — all 6 ritus
- Shishira (late winter): "Warmth and nourishing warm foods; protection from cold winds; light abhyanga."
- Vasanta (spring): "Light, warming and slightly drying foods; reduce heavy sweets; gentle morning movement to reduce Kapha."
- Grishma (summer): "Cooling foods; adequate hydration; avoid peak heat exposure."
- Varsha (monsoon): "Favor freshly cooked, dry foods; support digestion with light spices."
- Sharad (post-monsoon): "Light warm breakfasts; re-establish regular activity and digestive support."
- Hemanta (early winter): "Nourishing warm foods; mild abhyanga; protect from cold exposure."

---

## 6. Psychometric phrasing
- Use neutral descriptive titles: "Anxiety", "Burnout", "Stress", "Resilience"
- Example: "Psychometric profile indicates predominance of anxiety features; short-term calming practices recommended."

---

## 7. Career suggestion template
- Use: "Role — Reasoning (Prakriti hint; Vikriti hint; Psych hint)."
- Example:
  - "Clinical researcher — Predominant Pitta constitution; current balanced Vikriti; suited for analytical, structured tasks (score: 78)."

---

## 8. Doctor’s note guidance (highlight box)
- Short, professional, signed by clinician.
- Tone: "The clinician notes that ...", "In the clinician's opinion, ...", "Follow-up recommended in X weeks."

---

## 9. Examples (direct replacements)
- Instead of: "You should drink warm water in the morning."
  - Use: "Warm water on waking is recommended."
- Instead of: "You may feel more energetic in the morning."
  - Use: "Morning periods typically show higher energy levels."

---

## 10. Checklist for final QA (before export)
- No occurrence of the word "you" in the PDF text.
- Executive summary ≤ 4 lines.
- Charts have legend displayed or noted.
- Relationship & health lists merged (no duplicates).
- Doctor note present if clinician edited it on the dashboard.
- Footer contact line completed.
